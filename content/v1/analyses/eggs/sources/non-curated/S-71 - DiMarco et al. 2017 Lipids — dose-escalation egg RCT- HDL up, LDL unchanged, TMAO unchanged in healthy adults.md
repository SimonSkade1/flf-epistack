Dose-escalation randomised feeding trial in a healthy population, comparing 0, 1, 2, and 3 eggs/day (each dose ~4 weeks) layered onto a carbohydrate-restricted weight-loss diet. HDL-C rose and the LDL:HDL ratio fell with increasing egg dose while LDL-C itself did not change; plasma choline rose dose-dependently with intake while plasma TMAO stayed flat. Lipids are the paper's headline framing (title leads with HDL); the TMAO finding is a secondary reassurance measure relevant to slice 7 as well.
relevance_note: A dose-response whole-egg RCT showing the lipid profile improves (HDL up, LDL flat) with increasing egg intake in healthy adults — key pro-egg/null mechanism anchor; also relevant to the independent TMAO pathway (slice 7 may mine it via found_via).
