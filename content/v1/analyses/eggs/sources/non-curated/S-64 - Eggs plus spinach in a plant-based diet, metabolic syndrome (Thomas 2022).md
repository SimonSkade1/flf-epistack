**Summary:** Randomized crossover trial in 30 adults with metabolic syndrome (mean age 49) following a plant-based diet for 13 weeks, then randomized to a 4-week breakfast of 70 g spinach with either 2 whole eggs or an equivalent egg substitute (2-week no-egg/no-spinach washout between arms). Egg breakfast produced lower body weight and higher HDL-cholesterol than the substitute; plasma choline and zeaxanthin rose only with the egg arm. A companion paper from the same trial covers oxidative-stress/inflammation outcomes (Nutrients 2022;14(12):2548) and is not separately minted.
**Relevance_note:** Another metabolic-syndrome-defined RCT (independent cohort from Blesso 2013, same lab) finding a favorable HDL/weight signal for eggs within a plant-forward diet — adds to the PRO-egg/null weight of evidence but shares the same lab's motivatedness pattern as S-43.
