Rockefeller University metabolic-ward study: 75 twelve-week diet periods testing 35% of calories as saturated or polyunsaturated fat, each at low then high dietary cholesterol, with direct measurement of cholesterol fractional absorption and mononuclear-leukocyte sterol synthesis alongside plasma lipids. In 69% of the study periods, subjects compensated for increased cholesterol intake via decreased fractional absorption and/or decreased endogenous synthesis — direct mechanistic evidence for population-level "diminishing returns" and for wide inter-individual variation in the size of that compensation.
relevance_note: Directly documents the homeostatic-compensation mechanism that blunts the average LDL response to dietary cholesterol — the mechanistic basis for the "diminishing returns" pro-egg consideration.
