Uses the pooled ~29,700-participant Lifetime Risk Pooling Project (6 US cohorts: ARIC, CARDIA, CHS, Framingham original + Offspring, MESA) to model isocaloric substitution of one serving/day of one animal-protein food for another, estimating the resulting change in incident CVD and all-cause mortality risk. Substituting eggs with fish, nuts, legumes, or whole grains was associated with 2-3% lower CVD risk at one serving/week and 15-21% lower risk at one serving/day substituted; the same substitution matrix lets eggs be compared against red/processed meat as the reference food instead, so the sign and size of the "egg effect" is shown, within one paper, to depend on the comparator. Shares its underlying cohort data with the Zhong 2019 JAMA egg-CVD cohort paper (slice 1) — flagged for step 2's data_basis reconciliation, not a duplicate publication.

relevance_note: Direct primary demonstrating the comparator-dependence of the egg-health association — the core epistemic claim of this slice's substitution thread.
