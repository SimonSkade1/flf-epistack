**Endpoint:** surrogate — postprandial/short-term plasma TMAO.

Randomized, controlled, dose-response feeding study: healthy adults consumed 0, 1, 2, or 4 hard-boiled eggs. Plasma TMAO rose in a dose-dependent manner in the hours following egg ingestion, alongside choline and betaine, directly demonstrating that eating eggs measurably raises TMAO.

relevance_note: the cleanest "yes, eating eggs does raise TMAO" primary in the pool, with a proper dose-response design — the harm-pathway anchor for thread (ii). Note the rise documented is acute/postprandial, which the chronic-feeding null studies in this pool (DiMarco 2017, Lemos 2018, Wilcox 2021) argue does not translate into a change in fasting/steady-state TMAO.

**Correction (2a read):** Miller 2014 is an n=6 within-subject dose-response (0/1/2/4/6 yolks), not a larger-n trial.
