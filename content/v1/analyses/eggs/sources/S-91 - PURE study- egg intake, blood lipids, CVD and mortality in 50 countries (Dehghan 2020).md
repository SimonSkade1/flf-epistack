146,011 adults from 21 countries across 6 continents in the Prospective Urban Rural Epidemiology (PURE) study (low-, middle- and high-income countries, including several Asian and Middle Eastern populations); a combined analysis additionally pooling in the ONTARGET/TRANSCEND trial cohorts (31,544 more, other countries) is described as spanning "177,000 people in 50 countries" in the title. Egg intake ≥7/week vs <1/week showed no significant association with the composite CVD/mortality outcome (HR 0.96), all-cause mortality (HR 1.04), or major CVD events (HR 0.92) in PURE; the ONTARGET/TRANSCEND analysis showed similarly null associations. No adverse blood-lipid signal from higher egg intake either.

relevance_note: The largest, most geographically diverse null anchor in the pool — critically, PURE's country mix means this null is NOT just a Western/high-income finding, directly bearing on whether "eggs are neutral" generalizes across income levels and diets, not only across East Asia specifically.
