#### What the analysis says

The cluster asks whether eggs enhance satiety beyond what their protein content explains: [[H-5 - Eggs increase satiety and reduce hunger beyond their protein content]] versus the constructed complement [[H-47 - Egg satiety is fully explained by protein content]]; prior [0.35, 0.65] → posterior [0.486, 0.514], barely off a coin-flip. A single inbound observation ([[E-1 - O-7 x HC-13 — greater satiety under matched protein]], DIABEGG's greater postbreakfast fullness under matched protein) nudged toward H-5, but it discriminates only weakly (H-47 scored 0.45, trust 0.7) because the appetite ratings are unblinded and self-reported, so expectation effects or imperfect protein-matching reproduce the result under the null.

#### What the model may not capture

The two members are exhaustive by construction, so the answer is on the list — but H-47 was minted as a complement no source asserts, and one unblinded RCT is thin evidence for a channel (food-matrix, lipid, gastric emptying) that would have to beat protein; the near-50/50 posterior is honest about how little is known rather than a real disagreement in the literature.

#### What would help

1. A blinded or objective-intake (ad-libitum energy) satiety trial with verified iso-protein matching — *does not exist* in the curated set.
2. Replication beyond the single DIABEGG cohort — *unclear*.

#### Confusions and contradictions

None irreducible; the only tension is that a subjective unblinded outcome cannot itself distinguish a real beyond-protein effect from an expectation artefact, which the model handles by weak discrimination rather than by papering over.
