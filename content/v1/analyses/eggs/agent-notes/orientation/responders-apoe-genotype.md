## Slice

Inter-individual variation in the blood-lipid response to dietary cholesterol/eggs — the mechanistic "for whom" of the lipid pathway. 12 sources minted (budget was 10; went ~20% over because the three threads — response classification, ApoE genotype, other genetic/absorption modifiers — turned out to be three genuinely distinct, well-populated literatures rather than one, and each needed enough breadth to carry its own internal disagreement rather than a single cherry-picked anchor). Went looking for a fourth thread — a dedicated NPC1L1-polymorphism-and-egg-response primary — and could not find one (see Exclusions).

The pool holds a real, load-bearing disagreement that should NOT be smoothed over downstream: the two named ApoE anchor studies disagree with each other. [[S-65 - Sarkkinen et al 1998 AJCN - Effect of apolipoprotein E polymorphism on serum lipid response to dietary fat and dietary cholesterol]] finds E4/4 carriers most responsive to dietary cholesterol specifically (supports "ApoE4 carriers respond more"); [[S-62 - Weggemans, Zock, Ordovas, Pedro-Botet and Katan 2001 Atherosclerosis - ApoE genotype and response of serum cholesterol to dietary fat, cholesterol and cafestol]], the largest pooled dataset (n=395), finds NO genotype effect on the dietary-cholesterol response specifically (only on saturated fat and cafestol). [[S-70 - Lopez-Miranda et al 1994 J Lipid Res - Effect of apolipoprotein E phenotype on diet-induced lowering of plasma LDL cholesterol]] sits in between: a genotype effect in men, not in women. This three-way tension is itself the finding for this thread, not noise to average away.

## Topics

### (i) Hyper- vs. hypo-responder classification and its physiology
Best-first:
1. [[S-22 - Katan and Beynen 1986 - Existence of consistent hypo- and hyperresponders to dietary cholesterol in man]] — foundational: hypo/hyper response is a stable, reproducible individual trait, not noise.
2. [[S-25 - McNamara et al 1987 - Heterogeneity of cholesterol homeostasis in man]] — the physiological mechanism (absorption/synthesis feedback) behind the split; ~69% compensate, ~31% don't.
3. [[S-74 - Kern 1991 NEJM - Normal plasma cholesterol in an 88-year-old man who eats 25 eggs a day, mechanisms of adaptation]] — the extreme single-case existence proof of McNamara's mechanism (18% absorption efficiency, 25 eggs/day, normal cholesterol).
4. [[S-55 - Missimer, DiMarco and Fernandez 2019 Curr Dev Nutr - Egg-derived dietary cholesterol response, distinct lipoprotein profiles, carotenoids and choline unaffected]] — modern (NMR-based) replication in young adults; ~1/3 hyperresponder fraction stable across decades; separates the universal micronutrient benefit from the phenotype-dependent cholesterol effect.
5. [[S-30 - Herron et al 2003 J Nutr - Men classified as hypo- or hyperresponders exhibit differences in lipoprotein metabolism]] — hyperresponders' extra cholesterol raises LDL+HDL together, LDL:HDL ratio preserved (men).
6. [[S-32 - Herron et al 2002 J Am Coll Nutr - Pre-menopausal women hypo- or hyperresponders do not alter LDL-HDL ratio]] — same finding replicated in premenopausal women.
7. [[S-53 - Greene et al 2006 Nutr Metab - Plasma LDL and HDL characteristics and carotenoid content positively influenced by egg consumption in an elderly population]] — same responder split and "benign hyperresponse" pattern replicated in an elderly cohort (highest CVD-risk stakes).
8. [[S-36 - Herron et al 2004 Metabolism - High cholesterol intake yields less atherogenic LDL particles independent of response classification]] — even hyperresponders shift toward larger, less-atherogenic LDL particles.

### (ii) ApoE genotype as a modifier
Best-first:
1. [[S-62 - Weggemans, Zock, Ordovas, Pedro-Botet and Katan 2001 Atherosclerosis - ApoE genotype and response of serum cholesterol to dietary fat, cholesterol and cafestol]] — largest pooled dataset (n=395); null for dietary-cholesterol-specific genotype effect (positive for fat/cafestol).
2. [[S-65 - Sarkkinen et al 1998 AJCN - Effect of apolipoprotein E polymorphism on serum lipid response to dietary fat and dietary cholesterol]] — smaller (n=45) but cleaner design; E4/4 most responsive in both directions, directly contradicting S-62 on the cholesterol-specific question.
3. [[S-70 - Lopez-Miranda et al 1994 J Lipid Res - Effect of apolipoprotein E phenotype on diet-induced lowering of plasma LDL cholesterol]] — genotype effect present in men, absent/unclear in women; adds a sex-interaction layer to the disagreement above.

### (iii) Other genetic/absorption modifiers
Best-first:
1. [[S-72 - Herron et al 2006 J Nutr - The ABCG5 polymorphism contributes to individual responses to dietary cholesterol and carotenoids in eggs]] — a sterol-efflux transporter variant (not ApoE) tracking with hyperresponse; the central non-ApoE genetic-modifier primary.
2. [[S-74 - Kern 1991 NEJM - Normal plasma cholesterol in an 88-year-old man who eats 25 eggs a day, mechanisms of adaptation]] — cross-listed from (i); also the clearest absorption/synthesis-phenotype case.
3. [[S-25 - McNamara et al 1987 - Heterogeneity of cholesterol homeostasis in man]] — cross-listed from (i); the absorption/synthesis mechanism is itself the "other modifier" story before any specific gene is named.
