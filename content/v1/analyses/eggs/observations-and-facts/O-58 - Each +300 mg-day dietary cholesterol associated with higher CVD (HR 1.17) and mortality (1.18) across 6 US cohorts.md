statement: "In 6 pooled US prospective cohorts (29,615 participants; median 17.5-year follow-up; 5,400 incident CVD events, 6,132 deaths), each additional 300 mg/day of dietary cholesterol was associated, in a monotonic dose-response manner, with higher incident CVD (adjusted HR 1.17, 1.09-1.26; absolute risk difference 3.24%, 1.39-5.08) and higher all-cause mortality (adjusted HR 1.18, 1.10-1.26; ARD 4.43%, 2.51-6.36)."
source: "[[S-21 - Zhong 2019 JAMA - dietary cholesterol-egg consumption and incident CVD and mortality, 6 pooled US cohorts]]"

The dietary-cholesterol exposure result. Associations were monotonic with no significant nonlinear terms (P for nonlinear 0.19-0.83), i.e. no apparent threshold.
