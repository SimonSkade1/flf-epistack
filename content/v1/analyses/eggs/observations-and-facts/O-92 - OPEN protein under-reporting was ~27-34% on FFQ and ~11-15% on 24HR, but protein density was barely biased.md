Protein absolute-intake under-reporting parallels energy (FFQ worse than 24HR). The near-absence of bias in protein DENSITY is important: much of the absolute under-reporting is a scaling/energy effect that partly cancels in ratios, which is why energy-adjusted or density exposure measures are less biased than absolute intakes.
