## Why this is evidence

Under H-16 a single introduction that diversified into A and B requires an intermediate haplotype (carrying only one of the two lineage-defining mutations) to have genuinely circulated in humans, bridging A to B. O-5 finds the apparent intermediates to be sequencing artifacts, i.e. no genuine intermediate circulated, removing that bridge and favouring H-5, in which the two-mutation gap sits across the human-population boundary via separate introductions.
