A market spillover requires (i) a susceptible host species physically present and (ii) conditions of intense, bloody, high-density human-animal contact. Raccoon dogs and civets are experimentally SARS-CoV-2-susceptible and were the intermediate hosts in the 2003 SARS-CoV-1 civet-market spillover; here they were sold live in volume, caged and stacked, with on-site slaughter that generates aerosols and fluid exposure. Both conditions are therefore met, making a SARS-CoV-1-analogous market spillover mechanistically viable and raising its prior. Limit (for scoring): feasibility is not occurrence - the survey shows the opportunity existed but provides no infected animal, so it cannot by itself establish that a spillover happened.
