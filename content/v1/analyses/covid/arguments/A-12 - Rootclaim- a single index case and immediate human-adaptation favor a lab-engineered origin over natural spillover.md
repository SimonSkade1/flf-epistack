## reasoning
Unlike many past pandemics that involved multiple animal-to-human jumps, COVID-19 appears to trace to a single index case, and SARS-CoV-2 was already well adapted for human infection from the first known cases. Rootclaim reads immediate human-adaptation and a single introduction as more expected if the virus was cultured/engineered on human-adapted systems than for a fresh natural spillover. Factors: lab-escape ×2 and bioweapon ×2, with zoonotic and zoonotic-collection correspondingly reduced. Running likelihoods: Zoonotic 7%, Lab-escape 79%, Bioweapon 12%, Zoonotic-collection 2.6%.

## Validity verdict (step 6)
Reconstruction — Observed premises: (a) the outbreak traces to a single apparent index case rather than multiple independent jumps; (b) the virus was already well-adapted to humans in the earliest cases. Load-bearing step: each fact is more expected under a cultured/engineered origin (passaged or designed on human-adapted systems) than under a fresh natural spillover, hence favors lab.

Probe for an undercutting defeater. Prong (b) faces a real one: a pre-adapted animal reservoir producing a human-capable virus reproduces "immediate human-adaptation" without any lab involvement (cf. A-5), and the RBD's non-optimal look (A-58) cuts against deliberate design — so this prong is close to symmetric, only weakly lab-leaning. Prong (a): single-introduction events are common in natural spillovers too, so the asymmetry is modest. Both prongs are genuinely weak, but neither defeater flips the sign: culturing/engineering does more reliably yield an immediately human-adapted, single-source introduction than the generic natural spillover, so a residual directional asymmetry P(obs|lab) ≥ P(obs|natural) survives. Per rule 1 a weak-but-real directional inference is approved; the heavy discount belongs at step 7. The statement is already at "favor" (evidential) strength, so no correction of the verb is needed. status: approved; reason_if_not_false: checked (asymmetry and its defeaters traced directly).
