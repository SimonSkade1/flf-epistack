## reasoning
Rootclaim derives the base rate from the frequency of each incident type per year. Far more viruses have entered humanity zoonotically than via lab failures; several recent novel-coronavirus pandemics were natural in origin. There have been no known outbreaks caused by a novel virus that came from research, though there have been lab leaks caught before causing widespread infection — including one leak of a *previously-known* virus that led to secondary infections. There are no known cases of a virus being released deliberately in modern history. This yields initial likelihoods Zoonotic 78% : Zoonotic-collection 6% : Bioweapon 16% : Lab-escape 0.6% (rendered 0.7% after rounding in the running table). Note the counterintuitive assignment: the deliberate-bioweapon prior (16%) is set far above the accidental-lab-escape prior (0.6%); the huge posterior on lab-escape therefore comes almost entirely from the evidence factors, not the prior. This prior-setting is a load-bearing, contestable modeling choice that feeds the overall calculation.

## Step 6 assessment
Reconstruction. Premises: each origin-type has a historical per-year incidence (near-zero for research-caused novel-virus outbreaks; several for zoonotic pandemics; zero known deliberate releases). Load-bearing step: relative historical incidence of an event-type is a legitimate basis for its prior probability on this event (base-rate → prior schema). Conclusion: lab-escape prior very low, zoonotic high.

Evaluation. The base-rate schema is a valid inferential form, and the specific step "no known novel-virus outbreak from research → low lab-escape prior" follows from it. The standard challenge — that GoF research at scale is recent, so historical absence doesn't bound the current-era rate — attacks the reference-class/premise (and thus the *magnitude*), not the inferential step; it is priced as prior strength at step 7. The internal oddity (bioweapon 16% set far above accidental-escape 0.6% despite deliberate release never occurring) is likewise a calibration/truth issue on the numbers, not a failure of the stated inference that "accidental lab escape is a priori very unlikely." No undercutting defeater breaks the base-rate step conditional on the premises. Approved; traced the base-rate step directly, so checked.
