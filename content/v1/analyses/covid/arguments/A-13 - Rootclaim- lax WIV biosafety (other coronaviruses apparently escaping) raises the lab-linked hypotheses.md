## reasoning
There is reported evidence of lax security and procedures at the Wuhan Institute of Virology, including other coronaviruses that seem to have escaped the confines of the lab. Lax containment raises the probability of any lab-linked release (whether of an engineered or a naturally-collected virus). Factors: lab-escape ×2, zoonotic-collection ×2. Running likelihoods: Zoonotic 3.8%, Lab-escape 87%, Bioweapon 7%, Zoonotic-collection 2.8%.

## Validity verdict (step 6)
Reconstruction — Premise: the WIV had lax biosafety/security, with other coronaviruses reportedly having escaped. Load-bearing step: a lab with demonstrably higher leak propensity raises the probability that the emergence near it was in fact a lab release. This is a valid propensity update — conditional on the leak-prone premise, the two lab-linked hypotheses (engineered escape, zoonotic-collection escape) go up.

Probe for an undercutting defeater: none breaks the link while granting the premise. The obvious challenge — whether prior escapes actually occurred — attacks the premise's truth, priced at step 7, not the inference. That the same laxity boosts both engineered and unmodified-collection routes (not bioweapon, which needs intent) is exactly what the ×2/×2 encodes and is coherent. status: approved; reason_if_not_false: checked (elementary propensity update traced directly).
