## reasoning
COVID-19 is more contagious than typical flu but not as fatal as recent coronaviruses (MERS, SARS-1), and it broke out during a relatively peaceful period. It is therefore poorly suited as a traditional bioweapon intended to kill; if deliberately released, the purpose would more plausibly be economic disruption than mass casualties. Rootclaim applies a ÷10 factor to the bioweapon hypothesis only, leaving the other three hypotheses unchanged. Running likelihoods after this factor: Zoonotic 91%, Lab-escape 0.8%, Bioweapon 1.8%, Zoonotic-collection 7%.

## Step 6 assessment
Reconstruction. Premises: COVID-19 is moderately contagious, less lethal than MERS/SARS-1, and emerged in peacetime; a deliberate designer would more plausibly optimize a weapon for lethality/impact. Load-bearing step: an agent who deliberately built a bioweapon would more likely have produced a more lethal/weapon-optimized profile, so the observed moderate profile is less expected conditional on a bioweapon origin. Conclusion: down-weight the bioweapon hypothesis.

Evaluation. This is valid likelihood reasoning: P(observed moderate profile | deliberate mass-casualty bioweapon) < P(observed profile | non-weapon origins). The candidate undercutting defeater — a bioweapon aimed at economic disruption rather than casualties fits COVID's profile fine — is explicitly acknowledged and is exactly what keeps the factor finite (÷10, not ÷1000); it bounds magnitude but does not reverse the direction, since most bioweapon design intent targets lethality and the statement is scoped to "traditional" (mass-casualty) bioweapons. No defeater breaks the directional step. Approved; step traced directly, checked.
