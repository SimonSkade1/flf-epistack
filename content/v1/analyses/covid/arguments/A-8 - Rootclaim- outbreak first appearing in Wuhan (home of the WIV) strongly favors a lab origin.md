## reasoning
Large cities are common initial sites for zoonotic pandemics, so Wuhan is no more likely than any other large city under a natural-spillover model, and it is not a desirable bioweapon target. What makes Wuhan special is that it houses the Wuhan Institute of Virology, one of only a few labs conducting gain-of-function coronavirus research. Under a lab origin the outbreak location is essentially forced (the virus emerges where it is handled), so lab-escape's likelihood is left unchanged while zoonotic is divided by 20, bioweapon by 15, and zoonotic-collection by 2. Running likelihoods: Zoonotic 51%, Lab-escape 9%, Bioweapon 1.4%, Zoonotic-collection 38%. This is one of Rootclaim's largest pro-lab factors alongside the genome factors.

## Step 6 assessment
Reconstruction. Premises: (a) under a natural-spillover model Wuhan is no more likely than any other large city, and it is not a desirable bioweapon target; (b) under a lab origin the emergence location is essentially forced to where the virus is handled (Wuhan/WIV). Load-bearing step: therefore P(outbreak in Wuhan | lab) ≫ P(outbreak in Wuhan | zoonosis/bioweapon), so location coincidence favors lab.

Evaluation. Conditional on the premises this is valid likelihood reasoning — a large ratio in favor of the hypothesis that pins the location. The prominent rebuttal (Wuhan is a wildlife-trade hub, so it is an elevated-probability zoonosis site, not a random large city) is not an undercutting defeater conditional on premises: it directly denies premise (a). Because (a) is granted rather than doing all the work by itself (the lab-location-forcing step is still needed), this is not completion-by-force. Whether (a) is actually true is a truth/strength question priced downstream. No defeater breaks the step given the premises. Approved; step traced directly, checked.
