## reasoning
SARS-CoV-2 has a furin cleavage site — an amino-acid sequence causing the protease furin to cut the spike in a way that facilitates cell entry. This feature is missing in related coronaviruses, and its placement in the genetic code looks like an insertion rather than a mutation, which Rootclaim argues makes it less likely to develop in nature. Factors: zoonotic ÷8 and zoonotic-collection ÷8, while lab-escape and bioweapon are relatively favored (lab-escape running total rises 32% -> 72%, roughly a doubling after normalization; bioweapon 4.9% -> 11%). Running likelihoods: Zoonotic 13%, Lab-escape 72%, Bioweapon 11%, Zoonotic-collection 4.7%. This and the chimera factor are the two strongest genetic factors in Rootclaim's case.

## Validity verdict (step 6)
Reconstruction — Premise: the FCS is absent in the closest wild relatives and sits in the genome like a discrete insertion, so (implicit likelihood premise) P(such a feature | lab manipulation) > P(such a feature | natural evolution). Load-bearing step: a feature more expected under a lab origin than under nature is evidence favoring lab. This is a clean likelihood-ratio inference; conditional on the stated premise it goes through, and the direction is valid.

Probe for an undercutting defeater: the strongest challenges — FCSs arise naturally in many betacoronaviruses via recombination/slippage, and the "insertion" appearance is reproducible naturally (cf. A-61, A-69) — all attack the *truth* of the premise "unlikely to arise naturally," not the inferential step that rides on it. Granting the premise, no alternative breaks the reason→conclusion link. The magnitude (Rootclaim's ÷8) is a strength question priced at step 7, not here. status: approved; reason_if_not_false: checked (elementary likelihood-direction inference traced directly).
