# Orientation — Huanan market environmental & metagenomic sampling (slice A)

Slice: the primary record of what China CDC physically sampled at the Huanan Seafood Market (Jan–Mar 2020) and every metagenomic reanalysis of that record. 12 `source` nodes minted, forming two chains (a data chain and a reanalysis-dispute chain) plus two institutional-report anchors and one adversarial critique.

## Topics

**The primary sampling campaign and its data (the trunk everything else hangs off):**
1. [[S-16 - Liu, Gao et al. — Surveillance of SARS-CoV-2 at the Huanan Seafood Market (Nature)]] — the peer-reviewed China CDC paper: 74/923 environmental samples positive, 0/457 animal-source samples positive, ~87.5% of positives in the west/wildlife wing, 4 near-complete viral genomes recovered (lineages A and B both present).
2. [[S-15 - China CDC Research Square preprint — Huanan market environmental & animal surveillance]] — the same team's earlier (Feb 2022) preprint; superseded by S-16 but the first public numbers, and the reason the "no animal positive" fact was known ~14 months before the raw reads were released.
3. [[S-21 - China CDC raw metagenomic sequencing data release — Huanan market samples (NGDC GSA - NCBI BioProject)]] — the actual sequence-read dataset (submitted Mar 2023) that every reanalysis below operates on. Its own release history (a quiet GISAID upload in mid-2022, noticed by outside scientists in early 2023, prompting fuller release) is itself part of the evidentiary record on data transparency.

**The raccoon-dog/susceptible-wildlife co-location claim and its dispute (zoonosis-side reanalysis vs. lab-leak-side methodological rebuttal, argued out in real time on the same dataset):**
4. [[S-35 - Crits-Christoph et al. — Genetic tracing of market wildlife and viruses at the epicenter of the COVID-19 pandemic (Cell)]] — peer-reviewed version; stall-level co-location of susceptible-species mtDNA (raccoon dog prominent) with SARS-CoV-2-positive samples, plus phylodynamic consistency with market emergence.
5. [[S-31 - Crits-Christoph et al. — Genetic tracing of market wildlife and viruses at the epicenter of the COVID-19 pandemic (bioRxiv preprint)]] — the Sept-2023 preprint version of the same result.
6. [[S-24 - Crits-Christoph et al. — Genetic evidence of susceptible wildlife in SARS-CoV-2-positive Huanan market samples (Zenodo report)]] — the original within-days rapid report that started this line.
7. [[S-39 - Bloom — Association between SARS-CoV-2 and metagenomic content of Huanan market samples]] — the central methodological counter: SARS-CoV-2 read counts correlate at least as well with fish as with any mammal; only 1/14 high-raccoon-dog-mtDNA samples had any viral reads.
8. [[S-42 - Débarre — What we can and cannot learn from SARS-CoV-2 and animals in metagenomic samples from the Huanan market]] — rebuttal defending the raccoon-dog signal against Bloom's aggregation method, from a directly interested co-author.
9. [[S-43 - Bloom — Importance of quantifying the number of viral reads in metagenomic sequencing of Huanan market samples]] — Bloom's rejoinder, using other animal coronaviruses in the same dataset as an internal positive control that (he argues) SARS-CoV-2/raccoon-dog fails.
10. [[S-53 - Nickels et al. — open letter requesting retraction of Crits-Christoph et al. 2024 (Cell)]] — 22-signatory, lab-leak-aligned letter arguing the whole market-sample dataset (collected weeks after market closure/disinfection, and after the phylogenetically-inferred index-case window) is structurally too late to bear on the original spillover event at all, independent of what it shows.

**Institutional/official record of the market environmental data (outside the academic dispute):**
11. [[S-47 - WHO-China joint mission report — animal and environment (Huanan market) section]] — the earliest official numbers (585 samples/33 positive, reported to press by end of Jan 2020), a smaller precursor to S-16's fuller campaign.
12. [[S-50 - WHO SAGO statement on newly released China CDC metagenomics data from Huanan market]] — WHO's advisory-body confirmation of the Mar-2023 data release's headline contents, independent of the Crits-Christoph/Bloom dispute.

Ordering within each group is best-first by my rough quality×usefulness read; the Bloom/Débarre/Bloom trio is really one back-and-forth and I've kept it in chronological/dialectical order rather than re-ranking mid-exchange.

## search_scope

Web search (via WebSearch/WebFetch) starting from the brief's named canonical anchors (Liu/Gao China CDC paper + its 2022 preprint; Crits-Christoph raccoon-dog metagenomics + its Cell/preprint/Zenodo versions; WHO-China joint report; Débarre's reanalyses). From each anchor: (a) searched for the specific preprint→published-version pair to get both nodes and correct DOIs; (b) searched "author + title" combinations to resolve exact authorship/venue/date where the general query returned only secondary coverage; (c) used the Semantic Scholar API directly (`api.semanticscholar.org/graph/v1/paper/DOI:...`) for citation counts, since WebSearch summaries don't reliably surface them; (d) followed the dispute forward from Bloom's first paper → Débarre's reply → Bloom's rejoinder, and forward from the Cell paper → the retraction letter that appeared the same day; (e) checked NCBI BioProject and NGDC GSA pages directly for the underlying raw-data accession, since no paper's abstract states it cleanly. Kept a lock-step file check (`grep`) across other slices' already-minted sources partway through to catch near-duplicate risk (found only one: S-34, a different slice's excerpt of the same WHO joint report for its nationwide wildlife-survey numbers, not the market-environmental numbers I cite — see exclusions).

## exclusions

1. Worobey et al. 2022 Science (geospatial case map) and the WHO-China report's early-case/residential-location annex — read as shared hubs, correctly slice B's (human-case data), already minted there (S-17, S-18). Not re-minted here.
2. Xiao et al. 2021 (Wuhan wet-market animal-sales survey) and raccoon-dog/mink experimental-susceptibility studies — slice F's (wildlife-trade/host-susceptibility), already minted there (S-19, S-20). Not re-minted; my slice owns the market surface/sewage swabs, not what species were sold or which are experimentally susceptible.
3. Débarre & Worobey, "Confirmation of the centrality of the Huanan market among early COVID-19 cases" (arXiv 2403.05859, response to Stoyan & Chiu) — this is a geospatial/statistical argument about the human-case map, not market environmental-sample data; correctly slice B's territory (already minted there as S-26/S-32-adjacent). Excluded from my slice despite surfacing repeatedly in my searches.
4. Courtier-Orgogozo & de Ribera 2022 (Environmental Research), "SARS-CoV-2 infection at the Huanan seafood market" — proposes human-to-human transmission within the market (mahjong rooms/toilets/canteens) rather than analyzing environmental-swab data; out of scope for this slice (it's an argument about transmission mechanism, not a primary environmental-sampling record) and not squarely slice B's either — flagged as a possible gap for slice G/the consolidator rather than minted here.
5. Dong & Hiscox 2023 (Viruses), population-genetics analysis of early Wuhan clinical (BAL) samples "associated with" the Huanan market — despite the title, the sequences analyzed are from human patients, not market environmental swabs; excluded as out of my data-basis (closer to slice C, phylogenetics of clinical genomes).
6. The four recovered environmental genomes (A20/B5/F13/F54) and their phylogenetic placement are documented inside S-16/S-31/S-35 rather than given their own dataset node — I could not find a clean, separate GenBank/GISAID accession page for just these four sequences distinct from the bulk raw-read deposit (S-21); noting as a minor gap.

## Slice shape

~12 sources, genuinely two chains rather than 12 independent findings: a straight supersession chain (WHO 2021 → China CDC 2022 preprint → China CDC 2023 Nature paper → 2023 raw-data release) and a live three-round methodological dispute over one derived claim (raccoon-dog co-location) fought entirely on that single released dataset (Crits-Christoph × 3 versions vs. Bloom × 2 vs. Débarre × 1, plus an adversarial retraction letter). I went looking for, and could not find, (a) a clean primary citation for the original Jan-2020 in-market press-conference sampling numbers beyond what the WHO report reports secondhand, and (b) any China-side or Chinese-academic rebuttal to Bloom/Nickels — every reanalysis and critique I found on both sides is Western-authored; if a Chinese-government or China-CDC response to the raccoon-dog dispute exists, I did not surface it and it may be a real gap in the slice rather than a search failure on my part.
