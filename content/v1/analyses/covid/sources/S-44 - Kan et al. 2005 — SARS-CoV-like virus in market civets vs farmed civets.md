Sampled palm civets and raccoon dogs at a Guangdong live-animal market following a January 2004 SARS case, and 1,107 additional farmed palm civets across 25 farms in 12 provinces. Found SARS-CoV-like virus in all 91 market-origin civets and 15 market raccoon dogs tested, but in none of the 1,107 farm-raised civets sampled later — a stark market-positive/farm-negative split. Phylogenetic and nonsynonymous/synonymous substitution-rate analysis showed rapid viral evolution in civets consistent with adaptation toward the human-infecting genotype, with the market population's virus more evolutionarily advanced than an inferred ancestral "prototype" group.
relevance_note: the primary source for the "market amplification" pattern explicitly invoked in the zoonosis case for SARS-CoV-2 — animals are far more likely to carry/amplify the virus once concentrated and mixed at markets than on farms, directly informing how much weight to put on Wuhan-market wildlife co-location versus farm-of-origin.
