Reports first author Xiao Xiao's own unrelated routine monthly surveys (May 2017-Nov 2019, i.e. pre-pandemic and not COVID-motivated) of all wildlife-selling shops at 4 Wuhan markets (Huanan Seafood, Baishazhou, Dijiao, Qiyimen), documenting vendor interviews on species, quantities, prices and wild-caught-vs-farmed origin. Total: 47,381 individual animals across 38 species (31 nationally protected), sold almost entirely alive in poor-welfare stacked cages with on-site butchering; no pangolins or bats among the animals sold. Species relevant to SARS-CoV-2 susceptibility were present in volume: raccoon dogs (~38/month average), masked palm civet (~11/month), American mink (~10/month), among others.
relevance_note: primary empirical basis for "which live, susceptible wildlife species were actually on sale at Wuhan markets pre-pandemic" — the animal-side precondition any market-spillover hypothesis needs; also the source most often cited to rule out pangolins/bats as the market vector.
