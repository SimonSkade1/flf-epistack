Rootclaim's flagship public analysis, applying their standard Bayesian methodology (decompose evidence into independent factors, assign likelihood ratios/Bayes factors to each, multiply against a base rate) to SARS-CoV-2's origin. It weighs genome features (furin cleavage site, closest relatives), the Wuhan outbreak location, WIV/DEFUSE-related institutional evidence, and lab-safety practice, concluding roughly 89% probability of an accidental laboratory escape of a gain-of-function-derived virus (vs. ~3-5% each for zoonotic spillover, deliberate bioweapon, and zoonotic-with-lab-collection scenarios). This page is the object of Rootclaim's standing "$100,000 Challenge," which Peter Miller took up in the Nov 2023 debate. It is one of the "six independent analyses" spanning the case's "~23 orders of magnitude": Saar/Rootclaim's own entry in that comparison.

relevance_note: the primary record of the lab-leak side's own formal Bayesian case — the anchor Saar Wilf defended in the judged debate and the entry Rootclaim's later posts (response to Scott Alexander, debate post-mortem) both build on and defend.

## Summary (extracted nodes)

Rootclaim's method: start from historical base rates, then multiply each hypothesis by an assumed-independent likelihood factor per piece of evidence. Final posterior: Lab-escape 89%, Bioweapon 4.5%, Zoonotic-collection 3.2%, Zoonotic 3.2%. As a debate artifact this node rests on no data of its own (`data_basis: []`); its factors are extracted as prior-shaping arguments attaching directly to its four hypotheses, not as observations.
