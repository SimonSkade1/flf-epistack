Reports that Chinese teams collected and tested >80,000 samples from wildlife, livestock and poultry across 31 provinces (before and after the outbreak) with no SARS-CoV-2 antibody or nucleic-acid positives identified; also describes market-animal sampling around Huanan market (457 samples across 18 species, all animal-origin samples negative for SARS-CoV-2, while some environmental samples were positive). The report's overall probability ranking: intermediate-host-mediated introduction "likely to very likely," direct zoonotic spillover "possible to likely," cold-chain introduction "possible," lab incident "extremely unlikely." Recommends further farmed-wildlife surveys (raccoon dogs, mink, civets, ferret-badgers) as a priority follow-up.
relevance_note: the largest-scale animal-surveillance effort to date on this question, and its consistently negative result (no infected animal ever found, in market or nationwide survey) is the central fact the intermediate-host hypothesis has to explain away — while its Chinese-government-mediated, incomplete-data-access process is itself a data-reliability caveat step 2 should weigh.
