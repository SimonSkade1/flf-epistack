Argues SARS-CoV-2's genome is consistent with laboratory origin via serial passage or targeted recombination: proposes the virus is chimeric, with a RaTG13-like backbone and an RBD nearly identical to a pangolin coronavirus (MP789), plus the unexplained furin cleavage site; argues this combination is a priori improbable via a single natural recombination event and would have been straightforward to construct with 2018-era reverse-genetics techniques.
relevance_note: the central published articulation of "the genome looks chimeric/engineered," and the paper that triggered the formal BioEssays rebuttal exchange with Tyshkovskiy & Panchin.
