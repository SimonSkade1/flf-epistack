Peter Miller's primary written case that SARS-CoV-2 emerged zoonotically at the Huanan Seafood Market rather than via a lab leak: early cases cluster both geographically and genetically (two lineages) around the market, market environmental samples from animal-associated stalls tested positive, and he argues the lab-leak case requires an implausible stack of coincidences with no direct evidentiary link to WIV. Written before the Nov-2023 Rootclaim debate but is the position he subsequently defended (and won $100k defending) against Saar Wilf; the debate's live/written record ([[S-76 - The Rootclaim vs. Peter Miller COVID-origins debate, video record]]) and the judges' decisions extend and formalize the same case. He also links a shared spreadsheet of debate participants' probability estimates, i.e. is one entrant in the "six independent analyses" comparison (his own numbers given, by his own account in Rootclaim's response post, "half-jokingly" / "kind of trolling").
