An interim staff report, "An Evaluation of the Evidence Surrounding EcoHealth Alliance, Inc.'s Research Activities and the Federal Funding of the Wuhan Institute of Virology," the product of over 100 investigative letters, 38 transcribed interviews/depositions, and review of more than one million pages of documents over roughly two years. Concludes EcoHealth used NIH funds to enable gain-of-function-adjacent coronavirus work at WIV without adequate NIH oversight, that EcoHealth routinely evaded federal reporting requirements (consistent with the FOIA correspondence in the companion node), and recommends debarring EcoHealth and its president Peter Daszak and referring the matter to the Department of Justice; HHS formally debarred both later in 2024. A broader, ~520-page final Select Subcommittee report (Dec 2024) restates this material within a full pandemic-response review without adding new primary documents on WIV specifically.

relevance_note: The most document-extensive single institutional investigation of the EcoHealth/WIV funding-and-oversight relationship, superseding earlier Senate/State-Dept assertions with subpoenaed primary records.
