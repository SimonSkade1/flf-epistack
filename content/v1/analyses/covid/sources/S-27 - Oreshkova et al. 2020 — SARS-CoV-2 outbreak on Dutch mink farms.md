Reports the first documented natural SARS-CoV-2 outbreak in a farmed non-human species: respiratory disease and increased mortality in mink on two Dutch farms, April-May 2020. Interstitial pneumonia and viral RNA detected in organ and swab samples; viral RNA also found in airborne dust in mink sheds. On both farms at least one worker had COVID-19-compatible symptoms before the animal outbreak was noticed, and sequencing later showed farm-to-worker and farm-to-farm transmission.
relevance_note: real-world (not just experimental) confirmation that a farmed species sold live in Wuhan markets (mink) is naturally susceptible, can sustain an outbreak, and can transmit back to humans — establishes the mink-farm spillback/spillover route as biologically real, not just a lab artifact of dosed inoculation.
