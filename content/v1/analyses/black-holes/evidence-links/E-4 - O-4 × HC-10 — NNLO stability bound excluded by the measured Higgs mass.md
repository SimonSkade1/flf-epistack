## Why this is evidence
H-42 (exact stability) predicts the measured Higgs mass should land in the potential's absolute-stability region; H-31 (false vacuum) is what the calculation instead indicates — the measured mass falls below the NNLO stability bound, so within the SM-to-Planck-scale extrapolation the potential develops a deeper minimum. Directly disfavours H-42, conditional on no new fields altering the running.
