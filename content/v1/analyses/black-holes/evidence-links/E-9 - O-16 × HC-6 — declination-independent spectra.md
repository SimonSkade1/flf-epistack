## Why this is evidence
A spectrum shaped by a few foreground sources (a branch of H-37, and the explicit rival H-12 argues against) predicts declination-dependent spectral features; a numerous-source-population account (H-12, and extragalactic H-9) predicts the observed sky-uniformity. A dominant heavy Galactic component below the dip (H-21) would also be expected to leave more directional structure than the mild dipole seen.
