## Why this is evidence

H-1 predicts massive neutron stars are real and reproducible across measurement techniques; H-44 predicts the maximum mass falls below this value, so any apparent two-solar-mass pulsar must be a measurement artifact. A second such pulsar, measured by optical spectroscopy plus timing rather than Shapiro delay, is exactly what H-1 expects and what H-44 must explain away — and the different method means an artifact would have to arise from a different systematic (white-dwarf model atmospheres/cooling tracks) than in the J1614-2230 case (orbital inclination geometry).
