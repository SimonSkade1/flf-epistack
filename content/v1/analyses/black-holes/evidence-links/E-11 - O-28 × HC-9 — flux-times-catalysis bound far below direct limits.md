## Why this is evidence
This is the model-space translation of the Super-K null: it excludes the joint region (flux near the Parker bound) x (strong-interaction-scale catalysis cross section) that H-20 posits, by many orders of magnitude relative to direct flux limits. H-20 predicts the true flux-times-cross-section product sits at a level such a search would reach; H-41 predicts any bound however deep stays null. The bound's model dependence (solar accumulation, pion branching, capture efficiency degrading above ~10^17 GeV monopole mass) marks where H-20 could survive it, which is why the discrimination is strong but not total.
