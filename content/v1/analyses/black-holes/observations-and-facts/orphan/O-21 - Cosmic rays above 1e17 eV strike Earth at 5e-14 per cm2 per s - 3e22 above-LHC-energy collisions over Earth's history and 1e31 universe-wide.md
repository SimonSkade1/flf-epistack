Derivation chain (all arithmetic from the measured flux): a cosmic-ray proton on a fixed target reaches the LHC's 14 TeV CM energy at E ≳ 1e17 eV; Earth surface 5e18 cm2 x 4.5 Gyr → 3e22 collisions ≈ 1e5 LHC programmes (each LHC detector expects ~1e17 pp collisions). Sun has 1e4 x Earth's surface → ~1e9 programmes; ~1e11 Sun-like stars per galaxy x ~1e11 galaxies → ~1e31 programmes since the beginning of the universe, ~3e13 per second ongoing. Caveat (ref [6] of the paper): the counts drop by roughly a factor A if the ultra-high-energy cosmic rays are nuclei of atomic number A, since the spectrum falls like 1/E^3. The comparison assumes special relativity holds for cosmic rays at these energies (ref [4]).
