Two sub-threads: (1) observation-selection-effect correction to "we survived past natural experiments, so it's safe" reasoning; (2) one additional independent compact-star observational primary for the white-dwarf/neutron-star survival leg.

## Sources

**Observation-selection / anthropic-shadow correction:**
- [[S-79 - Ćirković, Sandberg & Bostrom 2010, Anthropic Shadow- Observation Selection Effects and Human Extinction Risks]] — the primary, general Bayesian formalization of the effect; explicitly lists collider catastrophes among the event classes it applies to.
- [[S-78 - Bostrom & Tegmark 2005, How unlikely is a doomsday catastrophe]] — earlier, narrower primary deriving the anthropic bound on Earth/observer-destroying catastrophe rates specifically; the Nature-published companion piece to S-79's later, more general treatment.

**Compact-star redundancy:**
- [[S-80 - Antoniadis et al. 2013, A Massive Pulsar in a Compact Relativistic Binary]] — independent (different system, Shapiro-delay + optical spectroscopy) confirmation of a ~2.01 M☉ neutron star surviving intact, alongside the already-pooled Demorest et al. 2010 measurement.
