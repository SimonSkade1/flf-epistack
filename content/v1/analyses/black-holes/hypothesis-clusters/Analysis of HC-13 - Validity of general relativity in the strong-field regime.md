## What the analysis says

`H-13` GR valid vs `H-45` GR fails — a proposition and its negation. Prior `[0.84, 0.16]` (Laplace over ~8 passed regimes, 1.6x uplift for scalarization being motivated at this scale). One block, E-13: PSR J0348+0432 orbital decay matches GR (1.05±0.18), likelihood `[1.0, 0.2]` at trust 0.92, giving posterior `[0.9534, 0.0466]`.

## What the model may not capture

The 1.6x uplift and 8-regime count are round guesses (my read), each swinging the prior ~2x. "GR valid" here silently means "valid in the channel this one test probes": orbital decay is blind to short-range fields (>1e-19 eV/c^2) and non-radiative deviations, so a surviving H-45 realization is a real residual — and the safety argument needs full strong-field validity, not just the radiative slice.

## What would help

Independent strong-field discriminants — double-pulsar timing, GW inspirals — constraining channels O-19 cannot see — *exists, unread*.

## Confusions and contradictions

None irreducible: the measurement and GR prediction agree cleanly; no counter-evidence in the set.
