#### What the analysis says

Is up-down-strange quark matter the true ground state at zero pressure — [[H-28 - Strange quark matter may be absolutely stable in bulk at zero external pressure]] (Witten's conjecture) against its minted negation [[H-39 - Bulk strange quark matter is not absolutely stable at zero external pressure]]. Prior [0.267, 0.733] (a ~30% base rate for exotic-ground-state conjectures proving real, discounted 0.85 for the narrowing bag-model window). Two discriminating nulls did all the work — [[E-2 - O-42 × HC-7 — fifteen years of null strange-matter searches]] (ratio 0.4) and [[E-3 - O-26 × HC-7 — STAR null strangelet search at RHIC]] (ratio 0.8) — landing at [0.1329, 0.8671], H-28 roughly halved to 0.133.

#### What the model may not capture

The binary is exhaustive but collapses a distinction the danger chain needs: HC-7 answers *absolute* bulk stability, while [[HC-8 - Runaway growth of a stopped negative strangelet]]'s antecedent is *(meta)stability* of a small, producible, negatively charged strangelet — and large-A stability (the any-A form merged into H-28) does not imply metastability at LHC-producible A. That small-A question lives cleanly in neither cluster. The `depends_on` link, HC-8's explicit conditioning, and withholding A-57's positive-charge argument here to avoid double-counting are bookkept honestly, but the stability *concept* silently weakens from absolute to meta across the boundary. The cluster's own flagged failure mode is real: both edges stay above ratio 0 because a ground state "nobody reaches" explains every null, so a shared insensitivity would leave H-28 under-credited.

#### What would help

A lattice-QCD verdict at physical strange mass — *does not exist*. Whether small producible strangelets are (meta)stable independent of any-A bulk stability — *does not exist* (what the danger actually turns on). An audit of O-42's heterogeneous searches for a shared blind spot — *exists, unread*.

#### Confusions and contradictions

None irreducible. Both edges point to H-39; the only tension is between the nulls and their shared escape hatch — a part-2 structural point, not a live contradiction.

#### External consensus

Mainstream dense-QCD opinion holds Witten-Bodmer unconfirmed and probably false; H-28's 0.133 sits at or slightly above that lean — defensible, since no first-principles result excludes stability.
