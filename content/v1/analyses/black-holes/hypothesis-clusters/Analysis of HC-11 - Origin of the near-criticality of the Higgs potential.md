## What the analysis says

Why do the Higgs parameters sit near the stability boundary: [[H-6 - The near-criticality of the Higgs potential parameters reflects an underlying dynamical or statistical principle]] (a mechanism) or [[H-43 - The near-criticality of the Higgs potential is an unexplained coincidence]] (accident)? Prior [0.4118, 0.5882] leans coincidence; the sole witness CG-8 (the near-criticality itself, ~3x odds toward H-6) moves it to posterior [0.6135, 0.3865].

## What the model may not capture

The verdict hinges on P(near-critical | coincidence)=0.3, which the block admits is barely constrained (the (Mt, Mh) measure is ill-defined) — a factor-of-few either way flips the side. Members are a claim and its negation, so mis-carving is not the risk; A-6 is: a lower top mass would dissolve near-criticality and hand the cluster to H-43, which the flat likelihood cannot express.

## What would help

An agreed prior measure over (Mt, Mh) — *does not exist*. A post-2012 top-mass reanalysis confirming the ~2σ near-criticality survives — *exists, unread*.

## Confusions and contradictions

None irreducible; the anthropic-vs-look-elsewhere tension is reasoned through in CG-8, not left conflicting.
