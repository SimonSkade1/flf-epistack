Computes the actual emission spectrum and total power output for an uncharged, nonrotating (Schwarzschild) black hole using Hawking's formalism combined with Teukolsky-Press perturbation methods to get the greybody (transmission) factors right, finding e.g. 81% of power in neutrinos, 17% photons, 2% gravitons for large holes. This is the paper that turns Hawking's qualitative thermal-emission result into a quantitative evaporation-rate/lifetime calculation — the number actually used to argue an LHC-scale black hole would evaporate in ~10^-27 s.
relevance_note: supplies the quantitative evaporation-time calculation (not just the qualitative claim) that Premise A's "it evaporates fast" conclusion depends on.
