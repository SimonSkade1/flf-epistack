Companion paper extending the massless-particle emission-rate calculation of Page 1976a (Phys.Rev.D 13, 198) from nonrotating to rotating (Kerr) black holes, computing greybody-corrected emission spectra and power output as a function of spin. Rotating holes are the generic outcome of a two-particle collider production process (nonzero impact parameter → angular momentum), so this is the more directly applicable evaporation-rate calculation for an actual LHC-produced micro black hole rather than the idealized nonrotating case.
relevance_note: extends the quantitative evaporation-rate calculation to rotating black holes, the generically-expected outcome of collider production — closer to the actual LHC scenario than the nonrotating Page 1976a result.
