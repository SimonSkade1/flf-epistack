Argues the standard Hawking-radiation derivation is invalid because it uses formally infinite-frequency wave modes at the horizon, which Belinski takes to be an inadmissible artifact rather than a feature needing careful regularization; proposes finite-mode reformulation and concludes no particle creation occurs, i.e. black holes do not radiate at all. A minority/outlier position within the theoretical-physics literature but a citable instance of an outright "Hawking radiation is wrong" claim, as opposed to milder trans-Planckian-caveat critiques (Helfer, Jacobson).
relevance_note: the most extreme published dissent — a direct claim that black holes do not radiate at all, which if correct would fully invalidate Premise A.
