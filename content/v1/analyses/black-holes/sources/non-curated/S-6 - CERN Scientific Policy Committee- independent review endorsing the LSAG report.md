A dedicated 5-member SPC panel was tasked by the President of the CERN Council with independently examining the LSAG report and the companion Giddings-Mangano astrophysical-implications paper. The panel, and subsequently the full SPC, unanimously endorsed LSAG's conclusions: "there is no basis for any concerns about the consequences of new particles or forms of matter that could possibly be produced at the LHC." This is the formal governance-level ratification step that elevated LSAG from an expert-group report to CERN's institutional position.
