Rössler (chaos theorist, U. Tübingen, known for the Rössler attractor) argues for a non-standard ("Abraham-solution") reading of the Schwarzschild metric under which a charged/rotating micro black hole produced at the LHC would not evaporate via Hawking radiation but would instead grow, oscillating in and out of the beam pipe and eventually consuming the Earth on a timescale he estimated as roughly 5+ years after first full-energy collisions. The argument was rejected by mainstream relativists (e.g. Hermann Nicolai, MPI Gravitational Physics, called it based on "an elementary misunderstanding of the theory of general relativity"); a formal commentary/rebuttal exists (Bruhn, TU Darmstadt). Rössler presented the argument at a cybernetics conference rather than a physics venue and was a co-complainant in efforts to halt the LHC by injunction.
relevance_note: The most concrete "danger is real" physics claim actually advanced in the public debate — its rejection by relativists is itself evidence about how decisively the safety case was received, but the paper is the primary artifact of the dissenting physics claim.
