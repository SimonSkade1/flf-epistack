First paper to work out semiclassical (geometric cross-section) black-hole production rates at a specific real collider: if the true Planck scale is ~1 TeV (per ADD-type models), the LHC would produce a black hole roughly once per second once above threshold, decaying promptly and thermally via Hawking radiation into a high-multiplicity spray of SM particles. arXiv:hep-ph/0106295. Co-founds, with Giddings-Thomas, the entire "LHC as black-hole factory" premise later inherited by the LSAG safety report.
relevance_note: canonical primary establishing the production cross-section and decay-signature that the entire micro-BH safety debate is about.
