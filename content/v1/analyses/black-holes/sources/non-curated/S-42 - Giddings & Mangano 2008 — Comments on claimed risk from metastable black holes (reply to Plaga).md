A short (~3-page) formal reply by Giddings and Mangano to Rainer Plaga's arXiv:0808.1415, which had argued that a *metastable* (not perfectly stable) black hole scenario could still pose collider risk because Hawking evaporation could stall growth at an intermediate size, evading their neutron-star/white-dwarf bound. Giddings and Mangano argue Plaga's scenario is internally inconsistent — that the same physics assumptions used to make the metastable BH avoid detection via evaporation also make its accretion behavior inconsistent with the astrophysical bound, i.e. they contend Plaga's "third scenario" does not actually open a loophole in their original argument. Does not introduce major new accretion physics beyond 0806.3381; functions as a rebuttal of the specific loophole Plaga proposed.
