`reasoning`: A single-front colonisation predicts a near-instant arrival horizon; a multi-wave colonisation spreads first-contact over a region and time. Under the multi-wave model, a ~2 kyr spread in youngest ages (as in [[O-14 - Youngest reliable ages for 8 of 12 dated genera fall within ~2 kyr of first human occupation]]) is *expected under predation*, not evidence against it. The inference is elementary — it only widens the window the observation is read against — so it was **checked**, not merely trusted. It enters [[E-14 - O-14 × HC-1 +A-7 — youngest ages cluster in the arrival window]] as reasoning that raised `p_ages_H3`, not as a multiplicative factor of its own (that would double-count).
