## reasoning
If eggs causally increased mortality through a cardiovascular mechanism (the proposed pathway via dietary cholesterol), one would expect a parallel increase in the intermediate hard events, MI and stroke. The PHS finds a clear all-cause mortality gradient (HR 1.23, P-trend<0.0001) but null associations for incident MI and stroke. This dissociation is hard to reconcile with a cardiovascular causal pathway; it is more consistent with higher egg intake tracking an unhealthier overall lifestyle (diet pattern, smoking, activity) that raises non-cardiovascular mortality, i.e. residual confounding from an abbreviated FFQ in a self-selected physician cohort. The exposure is crude (abbreviated questionnaire) and unadjusted for full diet, which further permits confounding.

## Step 6 verdict
**corrected / checked.** Reconstruction: premise = mortality gradient with null MI/stroke; conclusion as stated = residual confounding rather than causal harm. Hidden premise required: the only non-cardiovascular explanation of the mortality gradient is confounding. Undercutting defeater that survives: eggs could raise mortality through a non-cardiovascular causal route (e.g. glycaemic/diabetes-mediated, or effects on causes of death not captured by incident MI/stroke), which reproduces the same dissociation without any confounding. Two further defeaters against the strong reading: incident MI/stroke are non-fatal-inclusive endpoints with different ascertainment and power than all-cause death, so a null there need not contradict a fatal-CVD effect; and competing risks can mask an intermediate-event signal. The weaker conclusion — the pattern is evidence against a *cardiovascular-mediated* causal pathway — is immune to these, so the statement is corrected to that form. The premise's truth (whether PHS really is confounded) is not judged here.

## Original
The pattern of raised all-cause mortality with no accompanying MI or stroke association argues the egg-mortality link reflects residual confounding rather than a causal cardiovascular harm of eggs.
