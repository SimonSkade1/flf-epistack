**summary** - Independent, contemporaneous treatment of TeV-gravity black-hole production and decay at colliders ("black hole factories"), deriving production cross-sections and Hawking-evaporation decay signatures (many hard jets/leptons, roughly 5:1 hadronic-to-leptonic ratio) and arguing this would cloak short-distance physics behind horizons, capping how far collider physics can probe small distances. Cross-checks Dimopoulos-Landsberg's production estimate with an independent derivation reaching the same qualitative conclusion.

**relevance_note** - The second independent founding derivation that TeV black holes would be produced and would evaporate - corroborates rather than duplicates Dimopoulos-Landsberg.
