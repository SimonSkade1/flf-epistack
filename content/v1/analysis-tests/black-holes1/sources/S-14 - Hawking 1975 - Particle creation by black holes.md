**summary** - Hawking's foundational semiclassical-gravity result: black holes radiate thermally (temperature inversely proportional to mass) and evaporate at an accelerating rate as they shrink, so a microscopic (TeV-mass) black hole, if formed, would evaporate essentially instantly. This is the theoretical basis for "any LHC black hole decays before it can do anything" - the load-bearing mechanism behind most of the safety case. It is theoretically robust (derived independently by several methods since 1975, broad consensus among theorists) but has never been directly observed: no experiment has had the sensitivity to detect Hawking radiation from any real black hole, macroscopic or (necessarily) hypothetical-microscopic. That non-observation is precisely why the safety case needs a second, independent backstop for the case where a produced object turned out to be stable instead: empirical survival bounds from cosmic rays and astrophysical bodies.

**relevance_note** - The central, unobserved theoretical hinge: if Hawking evaporation failed to apply here, the safety case's main branch collapses onto the empirical-survival backstop.
