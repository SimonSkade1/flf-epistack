The census is a direct presence/absence measure. If direct sale of bats or pangolins at Wuhan markets were the transmission route, those taxa would have to appear in a 47,381-animal survey; their total absence makes that specific direct-sale route unlikely. But the market-spillover hypothesis does not require bats/pangolins on site: it runs through susceptible farmed/traded mammals (raccoon dogs, hog badgers) that the same census confirms were present and were themselves plausibly infected upstream in the fur/wildlife supply chain. So the absence discriminates among sub-variants of the zoonosis hypothesis (narrowing to an intermediate-host route) rather than counting against market zoonosis as a whole.
