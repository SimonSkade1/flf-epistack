SARS-CoV-2 carries a furin cleavage site at the spike S1/S2 junction that is absent from RaTG13, the BANAL viruses, and other known close sarbecoviruses. Under a pure-natural model this feature must have arisen by unsampled natural recombination/mutation. The DEFUSE proposal shows the specific idea "insert a (human-adapted) furin cleavage site into a SARS-related bat coronavirus spike" was concretely conceived, at the WIV/EcoHealth nexus, ~1.5 years before SARS-CoV-2 emerged. As a likelihood argument: P(observing a virus with this otherwise-unexplained feature | lab-origin at these institutions) is raised by documented intent+capability to build exactly it, so the match increases the posterior for H-5. Two limits cap the update: (1) DARPA rejected the proposal and no leaked document shows the work was performed via another funder, so intent does not equal execution; (2) intent+capability is not construction evidence, and the furin site could still be natural. The proposal is a strong motive-and-means match, not a demonstration that the experiment occurred.
