`reasoning`: If aridification of the observed magnitude were sufficient to drive the extinction, we would expect similar pulses at earlier, comparably-arid terminations; the review reports none. This is a base-rate (prior-shaping) argument, so it lives on [[H-21 - Climate-driven aridification drove the extinction]]'s `arguments` list, not on an evidence edge — it shifts the climate member's prior *independently of any single observation* (step 8 reads it there).

Marked **trusted, not checked**: it is an absence-of-record claim, and the earlier terminations are far more poorly dated than the 45-40 ka window, so the load-bearing step (that no pulse occurred, versus that one occurred but is undated) could not be fully traced at reasonable cost. [[CR-1 - Review of HC-1, dominant driver of the extinction pulse]] flags this as the cluster's largest unchecked inference.
