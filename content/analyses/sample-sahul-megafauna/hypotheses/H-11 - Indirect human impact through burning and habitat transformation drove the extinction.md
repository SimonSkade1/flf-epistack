The "staggered squeeze" answer: fire-driven vegetation change degrades habitat, and populations decline unevenly — most taxa track the burning front, refugial taxa persist. This is the **only** member that naturally predicts *both* [[O-14 - Youngest reliable ages for 8 of 12 dated genera fall within ~2 kyr of first human occupation]] and the survivor tail [[O-22 - Three megafauna genera persist more than 5 kyr after first human arrival]] at once.

Kept distinct from [[H-3 - Direct predation by arriving humans drove the extinction]] (not merged): [[O-22 - Three megafauna genera persist more than 5 kyr after first human arrival]] discriminates them — a >5 kyr refugial tail fits a slow squeeze, not a rapid kill-off.
